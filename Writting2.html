HOW I CAN KEEP MY CITY CLEAN AND GREEN{2014, BAGGED FIRST PRIZE IN SAKAL TIMES ESSAY CONTEST- DISTRICT LEVEL}[PUBLISHED – 23 FEBRUARY 2014]

Every morning the sweeper in our society collects garbage from our house. At least two big drums of garbage are collected from our building. However, every Sunday, the PMC workers have a holiday, as a result all the dustbins overflow and our society looks untidy.

I had no idea where all this garbage goes. My father told me that this garbage is dumped in the garbage depot at Urali Kanchan, a small village near Pune. This means we are throwing our waste in the village to keep our city clean. I read in newspaper that this garbage is creating health problems in the villages near Urali Kanchan.

Many people do not separate wet and dry garbage, which makes it hard to recycle it. If we keep wet garbage separately it can be decomposed and dry garbage can be recycled. In the film “STORY OF STUFF” I saw that we are making a lot of garbage by the way of packing materials, use of plastic and using ‘use and throw’ materials.

I have at least 10 pens that are ‘use and throw’ type.

I can simply reduce this waste by using refillable pens. During birthdays, I use gift wrapping papers to wrap the gift. Though I know that the wrappers are just discarded.So, doesn’t it make any sense to avoid wrapping gifts?

During Ganeshotsav, we can immerse idol in tanks, provided by government, which will prevent water pollution.In addition, we should avoid defecating in open grounds, as it leads to the growth of Congress grass.

AS A RESOLUTION THIS YEAR, I AM GOING TO WORK TOWARDS REDUCING 50% OF THE GARBAGE.
